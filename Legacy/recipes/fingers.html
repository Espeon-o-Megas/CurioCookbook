Roasted Severed Fingers!

Warning! Some aspiring cooks may find the ingredients of this recipe to be... unconventional.

Welcome to our second-ever recipe! What follows is a savory, spice-packed umami bomb perfect as a game night snack or an entree. No matter the mood, one thing is certain. Your guests will be licking their fingers during and after their meal! Ha!

"Shopping" List

This is all the stuff you need to get fingering!

  • Severed fingers (preferrably human or simian)
  • Dark and light soy sauce
  • Honey
  • Sancified Szechuan Pepper (found in most asian supermarkets)
  • 3000-year-old rice wine vinegar

Getting your Fingers dirty

Here's what you'll need to do to turn your ingredients finger-lickin' good!

  1. Coat your fingers (not your own, the severed ones) with salt and let them dry brine -uncovered in the fridge- for at least one hour.
  2. After the brining process is done, toss the fingers in a blazing cast iron pan along with some oil and sear until they develop a nice char.
  3. Take the fingers off the pan with a pair of tongs and then go in with your liquid ingredients (soys, honey and vinegar). Let them cook down until slightly reduced while scraping all the leftover finger fond to emulsify the sauce.
  4. Get the fingers back in the pan, coat them with the sauce and let them simmer until softened.
  5. Serve on their own, dusted with SSP or accompanied by a Gouged Eye Spicy Dip!

And that's that! Stuff these fingers in your mouthhole and feel the flesh peel off the bone! You'll thank us for it!
